Week 4

July 21, 2019

We're beyond excited to have accomplished the main goal of the week: making a functional headband prototype and display!

The headband is wired up and ready to interface with our classifier.

Originally, the headband was designed to have a circular cross section as circles are mechanically very strong. This meant that the headband didn't want to flex and fit nicely around our heads. To fix this we changed the shape to be an oval.

For the next version we want to work on the attachment between the two shells. Perhaps some clips might make assembly a lot easier.

Here you see the output of our classifer. In dark purple we have instances of EMG or blink artifacts, which would be rejected before the data is fed to a classifier. For illustration purposes we kept those trials and predicted on the full trace.

The model was trained on other sessions, so this entire 30 minute trace has never been seen before by the classifier. Pretty awesome that it could detect as soon as I stopped coding!

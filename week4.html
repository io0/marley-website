Week 4

July 14, 2019

This week was a lot of signal processing and visualization for quality inspection and feature extraction.

Here you can see alpha waves (10Hz) during eyes closed and rest, and higher power in the low frequency bands (0-4Hz) during passive activities like watching a video compared to tasks like reading a paper or writing code.

Another trace, this one is a lot cleaner. You don't see the two lines around 30 Hz, which are probably from noise.

A question we had was whether or not the baseline varied signficantly across trials (above). Perhaps we can use some baseline normalization or scale according to the power at 60 Hz (mains line interference).

We fed the time-frequency features to linear classifiers (i.e. LDA, SVM) and obtained accuracies in the 90-96% range for 4 classes: artifact, unattentive (rest), attentive (focused), and high workload (coding, reading).

Which sounds nice, but there's still a lot to think about. How can the information be presented? Can we make continuous metrics instead of binary classes? One thing I'm worried about is whether I can train on one subject and test on another, so that will be my goal next week.
